Hardware of a Computer

A computer is not a simple piece of technology. Many intricate parts are assembled together in a specific way so they can all work together to work as one unit. There are multiple parts to a personal computer, also refered to as a PC, and this webpage is dedicated to explain what the main ones are and what they do. If you are confused at any time, refer to other sections of the webpage for more information about a specific part.

Motherboard

ASUS Crosshair V Formula-Z motherboard

The motherboard can be thought of as the "heart" of a computer. It is a board of circuts that contains slots for other parts of the computer, allocates them power from the power supply, and allows them to communicate with each other. Motherboards have a spot in the middle for the CPU, with some models having an arm that comes down to lock the CPU in place. Around the CPU socket tends to be screw holes for the CPU fan to be secured into the motherboard to keep the CPU from overheating. There are more slots for the RAM, GPU, and various cables. The power supply is connected via two ports, one 20/24 pin port and a 4 pin. There are various manufacturers of motherboards; some of them are ASUS (a model from them is displayed above), Intel, Gigabyte, and MSI.
